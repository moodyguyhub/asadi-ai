10-Minute Strategy Consult

Risk-Alignment Choreography — Print & Keep Visible

0:00 Opening Frame (Authority, Not Rapport)
"Before we jump in, I want to be clear about how I usually use these 30 minutes. I don't pitch solutions. I try to understand where risk is accumulating in your system, and whether it's structural or accidental."

→ Camera on. Still posture. Do NOT ask how their day is. Do NOT ask "Does that sound okay?" Do NOT mention your background yet.

1:00 The Calibration Question (Core Move)
"Where does your system currently have the power to do something irreversible without a human consciously approving it?"

→ Then STOP TALKING. Let them answer fully. Every senior leader has an answer. This exposes AI risk, audit gaps, automation shortcuts, and org/process debt.

3:00 Mirror + Reframe (Judgment Display)
"What I'm hearing is [X]. The risk isn't that it exists — it's that it's implicit rather than enforced."
"Is that something you're intentionally comfortable with, or something that just grew over time?"

→ You're naming unconscious risk, not solving yet. This is where CTOs decide if you're a peer. Use only ONE follow-up.

5:00 One Constraint Story (Proof Without Bragging)

→ Pick ONE example that matches their risk. No timelines. No product names unless asked. No "I built." Frame as: decision → tradeoff → risk removed.

If they mention audit / compliance:

"In one system I worked on, we rejected async audit writes entirely. If the audit log couldn't be written synchronously, the business action failed. It slowed things down—but it removed an entire class of forensic risk."

If they mention AI / automation:

"In another case, we had high-confidence predictions, but refused auto-execution. Every decision expired unless a human approved it. Accuracy wasn't the problem—liability was."
7:00 Invite the Real Question
"Given what we've talked about, what would you want to be more confident about in your system six months from now?"

→ This shifts them from curiosity → ownership, features → outcomes. Let them talk.

9:00 Close Without Selling
"There are two ways this usually goes. I can summarize a few governance patterns that tend to reduce this kind of risk, or we can go deeper with artifacts and architecture in a longer session. Either is fine—what's more useful right now?"

→ You offer a decision, not a pitch. If they choose the second option, the booking CTA aligns naturally.

Red Flags — Never Say
  1. "We can build this quickly"
  2. "I've seen this many times"
  3. "Best practice"
  4. Any vendor or tool name unless they ask
  5. Talking more than 60% of the time — silence is leverage
Caller Forks

Founder:

Emphasize irreversible decisions scaling faster than governance.

CTO / CISO:

Emphasize implicit vs enforced constraints.

Investor / Partner:

Emphasize downside containment and explainability under stress.
